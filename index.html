what is happening here

A Risset rhythm is the rhythmic Shepard tone: copies of one pattern stacked at tempo ratio r, all accelerating (or decelerating) exponentially. Each layer fades in at the slow edge of the window and out at the fast edge, so as one departs, another has already taken its place an octave of tempo below — the pulse tumbles forever and never arrives.

Why every output is seamless: the loop length is never a free knob here. It is derived as L = m·qᴺ·P·ln r / (f₀·(r−1)) (with an extra factor r for descent), which forces every layer to complete exactly m·qᴺ·P·rᵏ beats per loop — an integer multiple of the pattern length — so each handoff lands on a pattern boundary at zero amplitude. Rational ratios (3:2, 4:3) stay lawful because the qᴺ fold makes those beat counts integers. There is no reachable setting that is not a Risset rhythm.

Melody: notes are Shepard tones — octave-stacked partials under a fixed spectral window over log-frequency — so they have chroma but no register, which is why the piano roll needs no octave axis. Steps hold chords: tap any set of degrees in a column. Layer canon transposes each layer by that many steps per log-tempo slot: any value is lawful, because pitch here is a function of x alone and everything that factors through x survives the handoff. At one full period per slot, pitch scales exactly with tempo, and the gliss drone becomes the true Shepard–Risset glissando, riding the same x as the rhythm layers.

Tuning: the entire Shepard machine is parameterized by one ratio — the fold period ρ. Partials stack at ρj, chroma lives on the circle log f mod log ρ, and equivalence is ρ-equivalence rather than octave-equivalence. 2:1 gives ordinary Shepard tones; 3:1 gives tritave Shepard tones — set edo to 13 for the Bohlen–Pierce chromatic; 3:2 and φ are stranger creatures with narrow, dense partial stacks. edo divides the period equally, and the scale menu is literal MOS theory: a chain of the tuning's best approximation to 3:2, which at 12-edo of the octave recovers the pentatonic (mos 5) and diatonic (mos 7) collections for free.

Midi: drop in any standard midi file and the loom risset-ifies it. Note onsets keep their exact fractional beat positions — the time-warp map t(b) accepts any real beat, so nothing is quantized to a grid. Channel-10 percussion routes to the drum voices; every other pitch is collapsed to its position on the fold circle, because register cannot survive an infinite accelerando — bass and lead merge into chroma, played as Shepard tones whose ring-out tracks each layer's local tempo, and root becomes a transpose knob. Under a non-octave tuning the 12-edo circle is mapped proportionally onto the chosen period, so a familiar tune at 3:1 · 13 becomes its Bohlen–Pierce shadow. Selections are padded to whole beats, so the seam theorem holds verbatim; the original tempo changes are irrelevant — beats are ticks over division, and the loom re-times everything anyway.

The whole family: take any logarithmically perceived dimension, stack copies at ratio r, drift them exponentially, window in the log. The state space collapses to the circle ℝ/(ℤ·ln r) and the illusion is uniform rotation on it. Pitch → Shepard tone. Tempo → Risset rhythm. Spectral position → the barberpole filter (an eternally rising formant). Angular rate → orbit, an ever-accelerating rotation around your head. Grain density, loudness, and the visual cousin — the infinite fractal zoom — are the same construction. One honest caveat: the drone is excluded from wav export. The discrete events are exactly loop-periodic, but a true glissando is only ensemble-periodic — oscillator phases precess by a non-integer cycle count per loop — so no finite wav of it can tile without a seam. The loom ships the exactly-periodic stem.
